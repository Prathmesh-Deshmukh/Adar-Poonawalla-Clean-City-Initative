Long-term Vision

Pune is the first city to adopt the Adar Poonawalla Clean City Initiative. While this is a first step towards achieving the vision of making cities livable, the initiative's long-term goal is to ensure that more and more cities adopt it to make Urban India cleaner, greener, and healthier.

An initiative to make cities more livable

Adar Poonawalla Clean City Initiative is an environmentally sustainable initiative, undertaken by Serum Institute of India and Mr. Adar Poonawalla as a contribution towards social responsibility.


His vision for the clean city began in 2015 when he found garbage piling up and being dumped across the city. He realized that if India's image had to be improved, a drastic change would be required with good management, local government support, and substantial funding. He has pledged to fund in his capacity Rs. 100 cr for this initiative.

Role of APCCI

The Adar Poonawalla Clean City Initiative (APCCI) is a project that focuses on the collection of street waste in Pune and Mumbai. Some of the initiatives undertaken in this direction include:

  • Litter picking from foothpaths.
  • Clearing of chronic waste dumping spots across the city.
  • Installing litter bins at strategic/high-traffic locations across the city.
  • Support vehicles and manpower for the clearance of litter from litter bins.
  • Providing drinking water to over 6000 families in fringe villages of Pune city.
  • Information and outreach programs to educate citizens regarding the importance of waste segregation and disposal using waste/litter bins.
  • Activation programs in association with local civic bodies in schools, residential societies, marketplaces, amongst others.
  • Training and sensitization programs for waste pickers/recyclers on improved waste management techniques.

The APCCI is supported by Pune Municipal Corporation, and the cleanliness activities are coordinated with local corporators, NGOs, schools, colleges, and residential associations.
